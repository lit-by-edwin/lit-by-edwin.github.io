The Uber Launch Challenge, UberBBQ and Industry Nights!
The Uber Launch Challenge
The Question
In perhaps Uber ANZ Recruiting's largest event in history, The Uber Launch Challenge invites university students in teams of 2-3 to pitch an expansion strategy for a shared-rides Uber product. 403 teams have signed up, with 1023 students competing for the grand prize of fast-tracked invitations to final-stage interviews for the Uber Graduate Program 2018, Uber credits, and, of course, Uber swag.

There will be 8 local campus heats beginning this Thursday; first place team from each local heat will move on to compete in the final round on May 22nd, at Sydney HQ. Mark your calendars!



UberBBQ
We partnered with Melbourne Commerce Students' Society to bring the students of UMelb "BBQ on Demand!" The DJ rocked the crowd and Donut Time had us covered on the snacks. Shoutout to Shilpa and her crew of experts, as well as Daniel Odoi, Dean Apostopoulos, James Burt, Jerry Zhou, Linnea Knutsson, Fletcher Coull, Alannah O'Kane, Ed O'Brien, Lucas Groeneveld for showing their support and educating keen students on what it's like to work at Uber.



University of Sydney AMME Industry Night
On 10 April, David Rohrsheim spoke and networked with 200 bright and keen Engineering Students from the University of Sydney. Accompanied by Geena Shin, Megan Beale, Daniel Blay and Edwin Ho from the recruiting team, Dave generated a tonne of hype around Uber's Grad Program, with many strong leads.



University of Sydney Start Up Careers Fair
The Start-up Careers Fair featured Uber along side early stage start-ups and incubators pitching young hopefuls on a career at the fastest growing start-up in Silicon Valley. Yes, we did get asked, "Are you still a start-up?" multiple times. Shoutout to Viv Tan and Harpreet Singh for reppin Uber Tees and educating the next generation on their future careers at Uber.



Network of Women Career's Conference
On 13 April, Uber attended the Network of Women's Career Conference at the Women's College, University of Sydney. We had a strong impact here, connecting with some brilliant female students and spreading a lot of interest in Uber as an employer. Shoutout to Erica D'Silva, Clare Leighton and Megan Beale for repping in an awesome crowd.
